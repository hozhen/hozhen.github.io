The other two pictures below the awesome radio flyer are the Wednesday event of Peecycler. What happened is that the day’s EWRE seminar we have guest speak from the wonderful Rich Earth Institute. and so I think with research requiring human urine. The department just decided to host an event in the center of everything to collect pee for science. Things to take away from the take is that human urine fertilizer is simply the side product of the bigger goal. The larger picture behind collecting human urine is to separate it from the waste water treatment system. What they found was that almost over half the nutrients including Phosphorus and Nitrogen are from human waste. And it takes a huge amount of energy and resource to isolate them when diluted in the waste water system. And so by preventing them from entering the waste water system. We’re potentially saving the energy and resource from being spent in treating them. Also something interesting I learned during the peecycler day adventure. Apparently one will feel like going when the bladder is filled to the 300ml mark (though this varies from person to person.) but the average flushing capacity at most urinal is 1 gal (3780ml) which in turns are about 10 times dilution. So for the human body equilibrium, what comes in must go out. For the 2 liters of water per average day, about 20L are used towards getting rid of the waste. There’s a lot of water that can be saved here. And so isolating the human waste from the current waste water system we can fertilize the crop, save energy and resource at the waste water treatment facilities, and save water for the same user. Although this may sounds ridiculous to people who are on the east coast, it can be agree that this well be greatly appreciate in the current California situation. #Peecycler. Save water!!

Another thing that happened this week was Dr.Raskin’s research group meeting. This Friday, Mr. Reddy and James was the presenter. I think it’s a fascinating thing to be working on Arsenic removal in the Bangladesh region. And for your information, the scary orange colored flowing in most of the Arsenic project picture you see around EWRE is the backwash process of the filtration tank. The Ingenuity here is the simplicity of the system. For the filter system we saw, water is pumped up, through the filter and into the storage tank. And so during the backwash period all that’s happening is draining the tank, which in turns reduces the system maintenance cost.
